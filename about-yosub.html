About Yosub

Yosub is a prospective software engineer and web designer, and currently a Computer Science student at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

He has built a back-end system for a growing web application company, has 20 months of working experience at technology startup IDINCU, and loves web design, having taken the Typography course at UIUC.

Currently Yosub is working as an undergraduate researcher at Distributed Protocols Research Group at UIUC and on Apache Cassandra open source project. He designed a system that supports changing configurations of Apache Cassandra—for example, a partition key—while maintaining the cluster online. He experimentally verified that the system scales well with numerous records in the database, and that availability is high while reconfiguration is being executed. (https://github.com/YosubShin/morphous-cassandra)

In the meantime, Yosub is participating in the development of an open source embedded key-value database called RocksDB as a Facebook Open Academy program. He is currently implementing the BW-Tree (a latch-free variant of B-Tree proposed by Microsoft Research) as an alternative memtable implementation in order to achieve better read performance. (https://github.com/YosubShin/rocksdb/tree/btree-nodelta)

He also TAs the course Data Structures (CS225), leading 35 students in the lab sessions.
