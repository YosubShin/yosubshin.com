About Yosub

Yosub is a software engineer, graphic designer, and a Computer Science student at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign.

In Summer 2015, Yosub works as a software engineer intern at Google in Mountain View, California.

Yosub also works as an undergraduate researcher in the Distributed Protocols Research Group at UIUC under professor Indranil Gupta. Yosub designed a system called Parqua that supports changing configurations of Apache Cassandra while maintaining the cluster online. He experimentally verified that the system scales well with numerous records in the database, and that availability is high while reconfiguration is being executed. (https://github.com/YosubShin/morphous-cassandra)

Previously, he built a back-end system for a growing web application company with 20 months of working experience at technology startup IDINCU.

Yosub also participated in the development of an open source embedded key-value database called RocksDB as a Facebook Open Academy program. He implemented the BW-Tree (a latch-free variant of B-Tree proposed by Microsoft Research) as an alternative memtable implementation in order to achieve better read performance. (https://github.com/YosubShin/rocksdb/tree/btree-nodelta)

Yosub loves graphic design, and he has taken two core graphic design courses at the University of Illinois since 2014. He loves to work with tools such as Adobe InDesign, Illustrator, After Effects, and Photoshop.

He also previously assisted students in the Data Structures course(CS225) at UIUC, leading 35 students in the lab sessions.
